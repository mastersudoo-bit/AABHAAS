🧠 Sensory Processing Disorders in Children

🔊 Sensory Overload

What it is: Difficulty processing excessive sensory input, leading to stress or meltdowns.

Example: Feeling overwhelmed by loud noises or bright lights.

Support: Sensory-friendly environments, noise-canceling headphones, and calming techniques.

👀 Hypersensitivity

What it is: Heightened sensitivity to sensory stimuli, such as touch, sound, or light.

Example: Discomfort with certain clothing textures or strong smells.

Support: Sensory integration therapy and gradual exposure techniques.

🖐️ Hyposensitivity

What it is: Reduced sensitivity to sensory input, leading to a need for intense stimulation.

Example: Seeking deep pressure or rough textures.

Support: Sensory activities like weighted blankets and textured toys.

⚡ Sensory Seeking Behavior

What it is: A strong need for sensory input, often leading to excessive movement or touching objects.

Example: Constantly jumping, spinning, or touching objects.

Support: Structured sensory activities and movement breaks.
