💬 Expressive Language Disorder in Children

📖 Description

Expressive Language Disorder is a communication challenge where children have trouble putting thoughts into words. While they might understand others well, they may find it hard to speak in full sentences, recall words, or tell stories clearly. 🧠🗣️

🔍 Symptoms

  • Using short or incomplete sentences 🧩
  • Limited vocabulary for their age 📉
  • Trouble finding the right words to express themselves ❓
  • Using “thing” or “stuff” instead of specific names 🗂️
  • Difficulty telling stories or describing events 📖

💡 Solutions

With the right support and practice, children can build their expressive language skills and communicate more effectively:

  • Speech-language therapy focusing on language building 🗣️
  • Reading together and talking about stories 📚
  • Encouraging storytelling and daily sharing routines 🌅
  • Using visual supports and vocabulary games 🎨
  • Modeling full sentences and repeating child’s words correctly 🧏‍♂️

📝 Action Plan

  1. 🎯 Identify signs and keep notes on your child’s speech.
  2. 👩‍⚕️ Consult a speech-language pathologist for evaluation.
  3. 📅 Schedule regular therapy sessions tailored to their needs.
  4. 📖 Read, talk, and encourage expression in everyday activities.
  5. 🌟 Celebrate progress with praise and patience!

✨ With love, guidance, and therapy, every child can find their voice!
