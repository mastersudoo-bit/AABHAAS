Conduct Disorder (CD) is a serious behavioral and emotional disorder in children and adolescents characterized by a pattern of aggressive or antisocial behavior. Children with CD often violate societal rules, engage in harmful behaviors, and lack empathy towards others. They may struggle with relationships, authority, and display a general disregard for the well-being of others.

This disorder can manifest as both external behaviors, such as bullying, stealing, or vandalism, and internal struggles, including anger, irritability, and emotional outbursts. Early intervention is critical in managing and addressing these behaviors before they escalate into more serious issues.
