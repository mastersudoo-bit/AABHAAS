🧠 Social and Emotional Disorders in Children

😟 Social Anxiety Disorder

What it is: A disorder marked by an intense fear of social situations and being negatively judged by others.

Example: Avoiding group activities or social gatherings due to extreme fear of embarrassment.

Support: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), exposure therapy, and relaxation techniques.

😭 Separation Anxiety Disorder

What it is:

 An emotional disorder where a child experiences excessive fear or anxiety
 about being separated from their parents or caregivers.

Example: Extreme distress when leaving parents for school or social events.

Support: Behavioral therapy, relaxation techniques, and gradual exposure to separation.

😤 Oppositional Defiant Disorder (ODD)

What it is: A pattern of angry, defiant, and vindictive behavior lasting at least six months.

Example: Frequent arguments with authority figures, defying rules, and being easily annoyed.

Support: Parent training programs, therapy (such as CBT), and consistency in rules and consequences.

🚨 Conduct Disorder

What it is: A serious behavioral disorder where a child engages in aggressive, violent, or antisocial behavior.

Example: Aggression towards people and animals, stealing, or destroying property.

Support: Multisystemic therapy, family therapy, and behavioral interventions.

😞 Emotional Dysregulation

What it is: Difficulty managing and controlling emotional responses, leading to intense reactions.

Example: Sudden emotional outbursts like crying, yelling, or rage in response to frustration.

Support: Therapy, emotional regulation training, and mindfulness practices.
