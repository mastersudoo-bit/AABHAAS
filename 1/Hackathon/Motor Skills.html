🌟 Motor Skill Disorders in Children

🌀 Developmental Coordination Disorder (DCD)

What it is: A condition where children struggle with tasks involving movement coordination.

Example: Difficulty tying shoes or playing sports.

Support: Occupational therapy, motor skill activities, and practice.

💪 Cerebral Palsy

What it is: A group of disorders affecting movement and muscle tone due to brain damage.

Example: Difficulty with balance, walking, and other physical tasks.

Support: Physical therapy, muscle strengthening, and adaptive equipment.

🧸 Dyspraxia

What it is: Difficulty with planning and coordinating physical movements.

Example: Struggling with tasks like writing or using utensils.

Support: Therapy to improve coordination and motor planning.

🦵 Muscular Dystrophy

What it is: A group of genetic diseases that cause progressive muscle weakness.

Example: Difficulty with walking or using arms due to muscle degeneration.

Support: Physical therapy, braces, and medication to slow progression.

🦵 Hypotonia

What it is: A condition where muscles are weaker and less firm.

Example: Poor posture, weak muscles, or delayed physical milestones.

Support: Physical therapy to build muscle tone and strength.

🦽 Spina Bifida

What it is: A birth defect where the spinal cord doesn’t form properly.

Example: Impaired mobility, weakness, or paralysis in lower limbs.

Support: Surgery, physical therapy, and mobility aids.
