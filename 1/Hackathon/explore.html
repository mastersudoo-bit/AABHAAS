🌟 Child Speech & Language Challenges

🗣️ Speech Delay

What it is: Slower development in speaking compared to peers.

Example: Not saying simple words like “mama” by 18 months.

Support: Speech therapy, daily practice, and reading time.

🔄 Stuttering

What it is: Trouble speaking fluently (e.g., repeating sounds or words).

Example: “I-I-I want that…”

Support: Calm speech environments, therapy, and rhythmic speaking.

🔊 Articulation Disorder

What it is: Difficulty pronouncing certain sounds clearly.

Example: Saying “wabbit” instead of “rabbit.”

Support: Sound drills, mirror games, and daily pronunciation practice.

💬 Expressive Language Disorder

What it is: Trouble forming sentences and expressing thoughts.

Example: Says “want toy” instead of “I want the toy.”

Support: Vocabulary games, storytelling, and sentence building.

👂 Receptive Language Disorder

What it is: Struggles to understand spoken language.

Example: Doesn’t follow directions like “Get your shoes.”

Support: Visuals, one-step directions, and comprehension games.

👅 Oral-Motor Issues

What it is: Weakness or poor coordination in tongue, lips, or jaw affecting speech.

Example: Slurred or unclear speech sounds.

Support: Mouth exercises, feeding therapy, and articulation training.
