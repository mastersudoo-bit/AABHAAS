👅 Oral-Motor Issues in Children

📖 Description

Oral-Motor Issues occur when a child has difficulty using the muscles in the mouth, lips, tongue, and jaw properly. These challenges can affect both speech clarity and feeding abilities. It's not about knowing the words, but physically being able to say them clearly. 🧠➡️👄

🔍 Symptoms

  • Slurred or unclear speech 🎙️
  • Drooling past toddler age 💧
  • Difficulty chewing or swallowing 🍽️
  • Struggles to move tongue or lips precisely 👅💪
  • Frustration when trying to speak 😣

💡 Solutions

Oral-Motor Issues can be managed with exercises, therapy, and consistent home practices:

  • Speech therapy focused on motor planning 🗣️🏋️‍♀️
  • Oral-motor exercises like blowing, licking, and chewing 💨🍭
  • Use of feeding therapy to build mouth strength 🍼🍽️
  • Mirror games for visual feedback 🪞🧒
  • Tactile tools like chewy tubes or straws 🧃🧸

📝 Action Plan

  1. 🔍 Monitor your child’s speech and feeding patterns closely.
  2. 👩‍⚕️ Consult a certified speech-language pathologist for assessment.
  3. 🏋️‍♂️ Start a therapy plan focused on oral muscle development.
  4. 🎯 Practice at home with fun, engaging oral exercises daily.
  5. 💖 Encourage and praise every small improvement to build confidence.

🌟 With patience and the right support, children can strengthen their speech muscles and express themselves clearly!
